Coco Wu, graduated from the Chinese Academy of Sciences-Institute of Computing Technology in 2010 with a Ph.D., and was a visiting scholar at the University of New South Wales (UNSW) in Australia in 2008. He is currently an associate professor at Shenzhen Information Vocational and Technical College, an external expert from the State Cryptography Administration, and a naturalist in Guangdong Province. He is an evaluation expert of the Science Foundation project and a member of the expert database of Shenzhen Science and Technology Innovation Committee. Research areas include blockchain technology, side channel analysis technology of cryptographic chips. As an important member, he participated in a number of research projects in the field of cryptographic chips, such as the 863 Program and the National Natural Science Foundation of China. He presided over 2 projects of the Natural Science Foundation of Guangdong Province and 2 basic research projects in Shenzhen. Published 31 papers and applied for 10 invention patents.


Research Direction
  1. Research on the evaluation criteria for the degree of decentralization of blockchain systems
  2. Research on early warning and evaluation mechanism of blockchain system transaction behavior based on graph theory topology
  3. Algorithm Research on Key Generation Mechanism and One Time Key Mechanism Based on Blockchain Public Chain Data
  4. Research on Computational Efficiency and Security of Cryptographic Algorithm Engine in Financial Security Chip
